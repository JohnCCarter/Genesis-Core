## Canonical bounded synthesis

### 1. Edge Topology is already at least two-axis on the annual surface

The lane closes with one strong baseline conclusion:

- **absolute performance** and **control-relative contribution** are different coordinates

`2024` proves that a period can be absolute-top while still living on the harmful side of the control-relative boundary.
`2022` proves the mirror image: a period can be absolute-negative while still living on the beneficial side of that boundary.

So the canonical map cannot be one-dimensional.

### 2. The recurrent annual pocket is real, but weak as a discriminator

The late low-zone, bars-`8+`, suppression-plus-substitution pocket is real and repeated.
It appears across all annual primary topology classes.

That makes it a real structural zone, but only a `WEAK` one in the registry.
It is part of the background shape of the system, not by itself the thing that decides whether the period is strong or harmful.

### 3. The strongest current anti-edge boundary survives, but only at the grouped annual proxy level

The clearest repeated harmful boundary on the current retained annual proxy surface is:

- the router still blocking baseline-long entries even after the blocked cohort no longer looks clearly weak on the same observational proxy surface

That boundary is still the only one promoted to `ACTIVE`.
After the 2026-05-27 active-boundary validation slice, the correct current reading is narrower:

- keep it `ACTIVE`
- interpret it only as a **grouped annual proxy boundary**
- do **not** interpret it as:
	- a clean single-carrier rule
	- a portable exact-surface transport rule
	- a runtime target
	- a global threshold law

So the label survives, but only on the grouped annual boundary that originally earned it.

### 4. Relative-beneficial annual periods are continuation-dominant; relative-harmful annual periods are suppressor-heavier

On the bounded annual primary cohort:

- `2025` and `2022` form the relative-beneficial side
- `2024` and `2019` form the relative-harmful side

The beneficial side is more continuation-dominant and lighter on suppressor concentration.
The harmful side is heavier on `RI_no_trade_policy`, heavier on `AGED_WEAK_CONTINUATION_GUARD`, and worse on drawdown versus control.

This remains a `WEAK` pair of zones because the cohort is deliberately bounded, not universal.
But it is already informative enough to belong in the topology summary.

The post-validation carrier state under the sole `ACTIVE` anti-edge boundary is also now clearer:

- mixed blocked action-layer explanation -> `WEAK`
- exact-surface `insufficient_evidence` D1 carrier -> `WEAK`
- broader local harmful `2024` shell -> `WEAK`

### 5. The month-level local seam is real, but it stays local

The frozen exact-subject triad confirms a real local monthly seam.
However, it also proves that the seam is not inherently positive or negative.

The negative local subject (`2018-03`) differs by:

- earlier release inside the cluster
- weaker decisive-row support
- longer path divergence from baseline

So the month surface contributes one useful local rule:

- **local release quality matters more than seam existence alone**

But this remains `WEAK` and local because the current month bench is too small for a stronger claim.

## Open contradictions and unresolved boundaries

### 1. Absolute-top can still be harmful relative to control

This contradiction is not noise.
It is now one of the defining properties of the topology map.

### 2. More monthly behavioral drift does not imply better sign

`2018-03` has the heaviest local behavioral drift among the exact month subjects and is still the negative one.
So raw drift magnitude is not the right monthly sign rule.

### 3. Several desirable layers are still honestly absent

The lane closes without pretending that the following are solved:

- general monthly `enabled vs absent` control
- flat-period structural coherence
- feature / indicator behavior map without raw feature reopening
- trigger-pattern / exit-family map without widening the retained period surface
