### Threshold-retained rows are close on score-like metrics to selected-defensive rows, but structurally unlike them on transition recency

Observed selected-defensive averages:

- `avg_ri_clarity_score = 26.0`
- `avg_conf_overall = 0.529631`

- clarity: `-0.5`
- confidence: `-0.002645`
- action edge: `-0.005289`
- bars since regime change: `+248.333333`

Bucket structure contrast:

- threshold-retained transition buckets: `stable = 12`
- selected-defensive transition buckets: `acute = 1`, `recent = 1`
- threshold-retained raw switch reasons: `defensive_transition_state = 12`
- selected-defensive raw switch reasons: `defensive_transition_state = 1`, `transition_pressure_detected = 1`

Interpretation:

- On clarity, confidence, and action-edge averages, threshold-retained rows look descriptively close to the tiny selected-defensive comparator.
- On transition recency, they look radically different: the threshold-retained bucket is entirely stable, while the selected-defensive comparator sits entirely in acute/recent transition states.
- This makes the bounded analyst summary for this slice **mixed** rather than purely near-miss or purely materially weaker.

### Threshold-retained rows are stable continuation-retained rows, not failed no-trade rows

- threshold-retained selected policy counts: `RI_continuation_policy = 12`
- threshold-retained switch reason counts: `confidence_below_threshold = 12`
- threshold-retained veto reasons:
  - `no_veto = 9`
  - `state_below_veto_floor = 3`
- threshold-retained zones:
  - `low = 8`
  - `mid = 4`

Interpretation:

- Most threshold-retained rows survive downstream veto and remain live continuation trades rather than being collapsed into no-trade.
- The suppression here is therefore primarily at the stability/switch layer, not at the downstream veto layer.

## Descriptive verdict

This slice does **not** recommend a new threshold rule. It only supports a bounded descriptive conclusion:

- The threshold-retained bucket looks **mixed**.
- It is **not** materially weak in the same way the min-dwell-retained bucket is weaker on the emitted score-like averages.
- But it is also **not** a clean near-miss clone of observed selected-defensive rows, because its rows are entirely stable and lack the acute/recent transition character that the selected-defensive comparator shows.

## Roadmap implications

This slice still does **not** authorize threshold tuning. It sharpens the roadmap one step further:

1. Threshold retention remains a valid bottleneck surface because it is the largest bounded comparator bucket.
2. Any future threshold-focused revision must account for the fact that these rows are structurally stable rather than transition-acute.
3. A separate min-dwell-focused slice remains useful, but it now looks more like a clearly weaker bucket rather than the closest proxy to selected-defensive rows.
4. Any next step beyond this audit remains only `föreslagen` and must be packeted separately.

## Residual risks

- This remains observational profile mapping, not execution evidence.
- The selected-defensive comparator is very small (`2` rows), so similarity claims against it must remain bounded and careful.
- The audit does not prove that any threshold-retained row should have switched; it only shows how that bucket compares descriptively to the bounded comparator cohorts.
