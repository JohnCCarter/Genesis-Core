## Defensive scarcity findings

### Raw defensive candidates are not rare

- raw defensive candidate count: `30`
- raw switch reason counts:
  - `defensive_transition_state = 29`
  - `transition_pressure_detected = 1`
- transition bucket counts:
  - `stable = 28`
  - `recent = 1`
  - `acute = 1`
- zone counts:
  - `mid = 17`
  - `low = 13`

Interpretation:

- Defensive scarcity does **not** begin as upstream raw-target rarity.
- The scarcity shown in this audit is scarce observed defensive selection within the raw defensive candidate population, not rare raw defensive target generation.
- The router sees a meaningful number of raw defensive states, and almost all of them come from the softer `defensive_transition_state` path rather than the stronger `transition_pressure_detected` path.

### Where raw defensive candidates actually resolve

Observed selected-policy decomposition for the `30` raw defensive candidates:

- retained continuation: `17`
- selected defensive: `2`
- retained no-trade: `11`

Observed switch-reason decomposition on the same `30` rows:

- `confidence_below_threshold = 17`
- `switch_blocked_by_min_dwell = 11`
- `defensive_transition_state = 1`
- `transition_pressure_detected = 1`

Observed veto-reason decomposition on the same `30` rows:

- `no_veto = 13`
- `policy_no_trade = 11`
- `state_below_veto_floor = 5`
- `defensive_transition_cap = 1`

Interpretation:

- The largest observed suppression path is not downstream veto; it is retention in continuation via `confidence_below_threshold`.
- The second large suppression path is retention in no-trade via `switch_blocked_by_min_dwell`.
- Veto still matters, but it is not the main reason raw defensive candidates remain scarce at the selected-policy layer.

### Observed fate buckets

- `retained_continuation_confidence_threshold = 12`
- `retained_no_trade_min_dwell = 6`
- `selected_defensive = 2`
- `selected_defensive_veto_cap = 1`
- `selected_defensive_veto_force_no_trade = 1`
- `other_observed_fates = 10`

Interpretation:

- The explicitly named fate buckets already explain most of the scarcity story.
- The `other_observed_fates` remainder shows there are still mixed paths where raw defensive candidates resolve into continuation or no-trade through less clean combinations of switch/veto outcomes, so the bottleneck is not perfectly one-dimensional.

### What “selected defensive” really means here

Among raw defensive candidates:

- only `2` resolve to observed defensive selection
- of those `2`:
  - `1` is capped by `defensive_transition_cap` and still trades
  - `1` is forced to no-trade by `state_below_veto_floor`

Interpretation:

- Even when a raw defensive candidate survives selection/stability and reaches observed defensive selection, downstream veto can still remove or cap it.
- But the main scarcity origin still sits earlier, because only `2 / 30` raw defensive candidates ever reach observed defensive selection in the first place.

## Scarcity origin verdict

- raw target rarity: `false`
- selection or stability suppression: `true`
- veto suppression present: `true`
- primary observed bottlenecks:
  - `retained_continuation_confidence_threshold`
  - `retained_no_trade_min_dwell`

Interpretation:

- Defensive scarcity is primarily a selection/stability story, with veto acting as a secondary suppressor.
- The next useful research question is therefore not “how do we create more defensive raw states?” but “which retention layer is actually over-binding defensive candidates: threshold gating, stability hold, or both?”

## Roadmap implications

This slice still does **not** authorize a new defensive rule. It sharpens the roadmap again:

1. A future defensive-focused revision should start from the observed `30 -> 2` compression, not from the assumption that defensive states are rare.
2. The two most concrete bounded follow-up axes are now explicit:
   - threshold-gating pressure on raw defensive candidates retained in continuation
   - min-dwell retention on raw defensive candidates retained in no-trade
3. Any next step beyond this audit remains only `föreslagen` and must be packeted separately.

## Residual risks

- This remains observational fate mapping, not execution evidence.
- The fate buckets are deliberately descriptive, not normative; they do not prove that any specific retained candidate should have resolved differently.
- The `other_observed_fates = 10` tail means some compression paths remain mixed and may need finer decomposition later.
